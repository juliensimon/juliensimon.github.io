We need to understand that LLMs are not intelligent. They are not reasoning. Every time I see that, I want to cry or scream. There is no reasoning whatsoever. LLMs are trained to do one thing: predict the next word. This conversation we're having is not me thinking hard about the next word I'm going to say. This is not how the human brain works. That would make for a very stupid discussion. I would sound completely idiotic. So that's all they can do. And yes, it looks like magic, but that's all they can do. They don't understand the world. If I say the phone is on the table, it's very likely that if I say the phone is on the, it will predict table. But if I say the table is on the, it could predict phone, or it could predict floor, or something else. This is based on context, the fact that two words tend to be in the same sentence a lot. There is no understanding that the floor is at the bottom, the table is on the floor, and the phone is on the table, and the table can never be on the phone. If the model never generates "the table is not on the phone," it's not because it understands that it's not a real-life thing. It's just because "phone" usually comes before "table." The next step is teaching models about this. Yann LeCun, who you should be familiar with, at least by name, is a strong supporter of open-source AI at Meta AI. He has a very good keynote, widely available on YouTube and elsewhere, where he explains that until we bring real knowledge—visual, text, spatial, etc.—these models cannot be called intelligent in any possible way.
